## Create your first custom intro

This guide walks you through creating your first personalized Game of Thrones intro video from start to finish. You'll have your epic custom intro ready in just a few minutes.

<Note>
  No account creation required - just fill out the form and receive your video via email.
</Note>

## Step-by-step guide

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the creator">
    Visit [gostargazer.com/game-of-thrones](https://gostargazer.com/game-of-thrones/) in your web browser.
    
    You'll see the creation form with all the options you need to customize your intro.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your title text">
    In the **Title Text** field, enter the text you want to appear at the beginning of your intro.
    
    This replaces the traditional "Game of Thrones" title card with your custom text.
    
    <Tip>
      Keep your title concise and impactful. Short phrases work best with the dramatic music and visuals.
    </Tip>
    
    **Examples:**
    - Your channel name: "STARGAZER STUDIOS"
    - An event name: "THE SMITH WEDDING"
    - A project title: "WINTER IS COMING: A DOCUMENTARY"
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your end text">
    In the **End Text** field, enter the text you want to display at the conclusion of your intro.
    
    This appears after the map sequence completes, perfect for credits or additional messaging.
    
    **Examples:**
    - Credits: "Created by John Snow"
    - Call to action: "Subscribe for More Epic Content"
    - Event details: "March 15, 2026 | King's Landing Hall"
  </Step>

  <Step title="Provide your email address">
    Enter a valid email address in the **Email Address** field.
    
    Your completed video will be sent to this address once rendering is complete.
    
    <Warning>
      Double-check your email address for typos. If it's incorrect, you won't receive your video.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose your resolution">
    Select your preferred video quality from the **Resolution** dropdown:
    
    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="HD (1080p)" icon="video">
        Standard high-definition quality, perfect for most social media platforms and web use. Faster rendering time.
      </Card>
      <Card title="4K (2160p)" icon="crown">
        Ultra-high-definition quality for maximum visual impact. Ideal for large screens, professional projects, and archival purposes.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
    
    <Tip>
      Choose HD for quick turnaround and standard web use. Select 4K if you need maximum quality for professional projects or large displays.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit your request">
    Review all your entries to ensure everything is correct, then click the submit button.
    
    Your video will begin rendering immediately.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Receive your video">
    Check your email inbox for your completed video. Rendering typically completes within a few minutes, though 4K videos may take slightly longer.
    
    <Info>
      If you don't see the email within 10-15 minutes, check your spam or junk folder.
    </Info>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What happens next

Once you receive your video, you can:

- Download it to your device for editing or uploading to platforms
- Share it directly on social media
- Use it as an intro for your YouTube videos, podcasts, or streams
- Incorporate it into larger video projects

## Tips for best results

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Writing effective title text">
    - Use all caps for maximum dramatic effect (matches the original style)
    - Keep it to 1-3 words when possible
    - Test how your text sounds with the iconic theme music in mind
    - Avoid special characters that might not render well
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Crafting compelling end text">
    - This can be longer than your title text
    - Include credits, social handles, or website URLs
    - Consider the pacing - viewers will see this after the full sequence
    - Make it readable - avoid overly long paragraphs
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Selecting the right resolution">
    - **Choose HD if:** You're posting to Instagram, TikTok, or Twitter; you need a quick turnaround; file size is a concern
    - **Choose 4K if:** You're creating professional content; you'll display on large screens; you want to future-proof your video; you plan to crop or zoom in editing
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
